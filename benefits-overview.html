Benefits Eligibility

If you are a regular, full-time employee who works 30 or more hours per week, you are eligible to enroll in all of Thermo Fisher's benefits.

If you are a regular, part-time employee who works at least 20 hours but less than 30 hours per week, you:

  • Are eligible to enroll in medical, dental and vision coverage at different contribution rates
  • May participate in the Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs), 401(k) Retirement Plan and ESPP
  • Are automatically covered by the Employee Assistance Program (EAP)/Work Life Services program
  • Are not eligible for coverage under the life and disability insurance programs

Who You Can Cover

  • A spouse to whom you are legally married (including a same-sex spouse)
  • A domestic partner who is 18 years of age or older; partners share the same residence; neither partner is married or involved in another domestic partnership; and partners are not related as a parent, brother, sister, niece, nephew, aunt, uncle, grandparent or grandchild
  • Your dependent, natural or stepchildren (who are living with you in a parent-child relationship and for whom you have a court-appointed relationship) up to age 26
  • Adult children age 26 and over who are mentally and physically incapable of earning a living.

Making Changes During The Year

The elections you make now will remain in effect until December 31, 2018, unless you experience a qualifying status change that affects your benefits coverage.

  • Changes must be made within 31 days of the event, or you must wait until the next Open Enrollment period to reenroll or make changes. Any change you make to your coverage must be consistent with your change in status.
  • You may change your medical plan level of coverage however, you may not switch medical plans.
  • You can update your dependent and beneficiary information at any point during the year.

Qualifying Status Changes

  • You get married or satisfy the criteria for a domestic partnership.
  • You become legally separated, divorced, your marriage is annulled or dissolve a domestic partnership.
  • You have a child by birth or adoption – or a child is placed for adoption.
  • You gain or lose custody of a child.
  • A dependent dies.
  • A dependent gains or loses eligibility status under the plans.
  • There is a change in your or your spouse/domestic partner’s employment.
  • There is a significant cost or coverage change in your or your spouse/domestic partner’s employment.
  • You or your spouse/domestic partner becomes eligible for Medicare.
  • You move outside your current medical plan’s service area
